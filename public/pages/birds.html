With its varied and unique habitats, from the beaches and river banks to the mountainous slopes of the Western Ghats, Goa is very much a bird watchers paradise. The state is home to more than 450 species of birds. There are 24 species of birds that are endemic to the Western Ghats. Of these, 13 can be found in Goa. The most sought after birds in the state of Goa are found mostly in the Bhagwan Mahavir Sanctuary. These include the Ceylon Frogmouth, Malabar Trogon, Black-backed Dwarf and Blue-eared Kingfishers, White-bellied Woodpecker, Great Pied and Malabar Pied Hornbills, Nilgiri Woodpigeon, Malabar Whistling-thrush, White-bellied Blue Flycatcher, Forest Eagle-owl, and Indian Pitta.
